# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Clothing resellers — people who buy items at thrift stores, garage sales, or wholesale to resell on platforms like Depop, Poshmark, and eBay. They work fast, operate on thin margins, and measure success in profit per item. They don't want to spend 10 minutes writing a listing; they want to move on to the next one.

## Product Purpose

Revora writes marketplace listings so resellers don't have to. The user describes an item (brand, size, condition, notes) and Revora generates a polished title, description, and tags via AI — then calculates estimated profit after platform fees and shipping. Success looks like a listing created in under two minutes that would have taken ten.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, efficient, no-fluff. Revora is the tool that does the work without asking questions. Confident and direct. Feels like a seasoned reseller's edge, not a soft consumer app or enterprise platform.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS cream/beige with small all-caps eyebrows on every section, identical icon-card grids, and muted warm off-whites — the current design drifts here.
- Heavy developer tooling aesthetic — pure dark mode, terminal fonts, power-user density. Not the right audience.
- Influencer / lifestyle softness — pastel palettes, bubble type, decorative shadows. This is a work tool.
- Overly corporate B2B — Salesforce or Hubspot energy doesn't fit a solo reseller.

## Design Principles

1. **Speed is the product.** Every interaction should feel instant. No loading flicker, no wasted steps, no filler UI. Resellers are measuring time per listing.
2. **Numbers front and center.** Profit, purchase price, estimated margin — these are the reason Revora exists. They should be immediately visible and visually prominent, not tucked into metadata.
3. **Marketplace-native energy.** Revora should feel adjacent to the platforms users already know (Depop, StockX) — confident, culturally literate, not a bland SaaS tool dropped into their workflow.
4. **Confidence without ceremony.** No eyebrows, no decorative section labels, no filler copy. If a UI element doesn't earn its space, it's gone.
5. **Work tool, not lifestyle brand.** Beautiful and purposeful, but not decorative. The aesthetic serves the workflow.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Sensible defaults: text contrast ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, keyboard navigable, reduced-motion alternatives for any animations.
